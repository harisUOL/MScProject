About Simple Polygon Triangulation

This web application performs triangulation of simple polygons. Triangulation refers to the process of dividing a polygon into non-overlapping triangles, where each triangle is formed by connecting vertices of the polygon with straight lines.

Guidelines for Input

Simple polygon is a polygon that does not intersect itself and has a single, unbroken boundary. Please ensure that the vertices are provided in a sequence that defines the perimeter of the polygon either clockwise or counterclockwise.

  • Valid Input: A sequence of coordinates that form a closed loop without intersections, such as (x1, y1), (x2, y2), ..., (xn, yn).
  • Invalid Input: Coordinates that cause the polygon's sides to cross over one another or do not form a closed shape.

Once the coordinates are submitted, the application will generate the triangulated mesh and display the resulting triangles. This process aids in understanding the structure and properties of the polygon and is utilized for efficient rendering and analysis in computational tasks.

Users can provide input in the form of coordinates that define the vertices of a simple polygon.
